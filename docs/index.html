How it works

1

Browse the map

Open the app — no account needed. Pins are color-coded green / yellow / red by carry status.

2

Tap a pin to see why

Each pin shows the restriction tag (federal property, school, posted business, etc.) and whether signage is posted.

3

Mark a zone

Sign in to add or correct a pin. Any authenticated permit holder can update any pin — crowd-sourced moderation keeps the data fresh.

FAQ

Is this really free? What's the catch?

Free today, with no ads, no premium tier, and no tracking SDKs. The app is built and maintained by one CCW permit holder who needed it himself. There are no current plans to monetize. If hosting ever outgrows what one developer can absorb, the first options I'd explore are donations, a 2A-aligned grant, or a non-essential paid feature. Selling user data is not on the table, and any change will be announced openly.

What data do you collect?

None beyond what the app needs to function. No analytics SDKs. No ad pixels. No third-party trackers. Your location is used to center the map — it's not stored or transmitted. If you sign in to add pins, your email is stored for authentication only.

How do I know the pins are accurate?

Pins are crowd-sourced by permit holders. Each carries a last-modified timestamp, and any authenticated user can correct a wrong pin. The map is a starting point, not legal advice — always confirm posted signage on site.

Do I need an account?

No, not to view the map. An email/password account is only required to add or edit pins, so we can revoke abuse.

Which states are covered?

All 50 states. Restriction tags handle federal facilities (18 USC 930), GFSZA school zones, Texas 30.06/30.07/51%, California PC 626.9 + SB 2 sensitive places, New York post-Bruen CCIA, Illinois 430 ILCS 66/65, Florida 790.06(12) under HB 543, plus airport secure areas, K-12 schools, and state/local government property.

Is this affiliated with the NRA / USCCA / GOA / a political party?

No affiliation with any government agency, gun-rights organization, or political party. CCW Map is an independent project.

How can I report a problem pin?

Tap the pin in the app, then choose Report. Reports are reviewed and acted on within a few days. You can also block a user whose pins you consistently disagree with.

Can I delete my account?

Yes. Settings → Delete Account → type-DELETE confirmation. All your account data is removed.
