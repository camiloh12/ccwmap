## Draft

I'm Camilo Hurtado, a software engineer in Tampa, FL and a concealed-carry permit holder. I built CCW Map because I would often go to events with my family while carrying and would only realize once we were at the entrance that they had metal detectors. This meant I had to go back to the parking lot while my family waited for me to get back (not a fun discussion with the wife).

The post-Bruen legal landscape is genuinely confusing. Sensitive-place rules vary by state, by city, by individual business. Even careful permit holders end up unintentionally non-compliant because there is no good way to know in advance. The tools that did exist were paywalled, ad-supported, or hadn't been updated in years.

So I built the thing I wanted: a free, ad-free, no-tracking map that works offline, where any permit holder can mark a zone and everyone benefits. I don't require an account to view the map, because asking for a login to learn whether you're about to break the law is its own kind of barrier.

CCW Map is open to everyone, costs nothing, and doesn't have ads or sell data. If you carry, install it, mark a zone in your area, and tell me what's missing.

— Camilo Hurtado · camilo@kyberneticlabs.com
